Defying both stereotypes and mass incarceration demographics, a new study found that among delinquent teenagers, African-Americans are less likely to abuse hard drugs than Whites or Hispanics. Conducted over a 12-year period by Chicago's Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine, the study focused on the abuse of and dependence on drugs such as cocaine, PCP, opiates, amphetamines, and sedatives, as well as alcohol, among delinquent youth after detention.
