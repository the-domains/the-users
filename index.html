The 'war on drugs' has failed and actually damages public health, major study finds

The five-decade long international "war on drugs" started by US president Richard Nixon has harmed the public health and should be scrapped in favour of a process of decriminalisation, a major new report has concluded. Anti-drug policies and laws have had "no measurable impact on supply or use" and cannot be justified on scientific or public health grounds, according to the authors of study commissioned by the Johns Hopkins Ivy League university and The Lancet.
